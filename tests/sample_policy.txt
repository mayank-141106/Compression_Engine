COMPANY LEAVE POLICY - 2024

Section 1: Annual Leave Entitlement

All full-time employees are entitled to 30 days of annual leave per year. This leave is accrued at a rate of 2.5 days
per month of continuous service.

Employees must submit leave requests at least 14 days in advance through the HR portal. Failure to provide adequate 
notice may result in leave denial or disciplinary action.

Exception: In cases of medical emergencies, the 14-day notice requirement may be waived at the manager's discretion.

Section 2: Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees receive pro-rated annual leave based on hours worked. Employees working 20 hours per week are 
entitled to 15 days of annual leave.

Notice requirements are the same as full-time employees: 14 days advance notice required.

Section 3: Leave Carryover

Employees may carry over a maximum of 5 days of unused leave to the following calendar year. Any leave beyond 5 days 
will be forfeited unless approved by senior management.

Exception: Medical leave days may be carried over without limitation.

Section 4: Updated Notice Requirements

As of January 2024, all leave requests must be submitted 7 days in advance for periods of 5 days or less. For longer 
periods, 14 days notice is required.

This supersedes previous policies but applies only to non-emergency leave.

Section 5: Consequences of Unauthorized Leave

Employees taking unauthorized leave may face:
- Written warning for first offense
- Salary deduction equivalent to 2 days pay for second offense  
- Termination for third offense

The company reserves the right to deny leave requests during peak business periods including December, March, and June
unless there are exceptional circumstances such as medical emergencies or family bereavement.
